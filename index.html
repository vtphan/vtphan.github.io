I am presently an Associate Professor and the Associate Chair of the Department of Computer Science, at the University of Memphis.

Here I am with Bob, the Buffalo, at the Shelby Farms Park.

Years ago, I went to Brandeis University, Massachusetts, and gradudated with a Bachelor degree in Mathematics and Computer Science. I then went to graduate school in New York and got a Ph.D. in Computer Science in 2003 at Stony Brook Univeristy, under the guidance of Professor Steven Skiena.

I joint the University of Memphis in 2003.

I teach courses that explore problem solving strategies and methods for analyzing large datasets. These include Algorithms, Bioinformatics Algorithms, Data Mining, and Data Science.

My research interest lies in exploring techniques to analyze and understand large datasets, especially biological data. Lately, as I developed tools to assist my teaching, I started looking into platforms, techniques and pedagogies that teach computational thinking and problem solving to novices.
